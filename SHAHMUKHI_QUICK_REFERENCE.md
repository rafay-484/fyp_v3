## Pronunciation Tips

### Unique Sounds in Pakistani Punjabi:
1. **نئیں** (Nai) - "No" - Shorter, sharper than Urdu نہیں
2. **او** (O) - Verb ending - Different from ہو (Ho)
3. **اے** (Ae) - "Is" - Unique to Punjabi
4. **وچ** (Vich) - "In" - Distinct from میں (Mein)
5. **توں** (Ton) - "From" - Different from سے (Se)

### Common Verb Endings:
- **ہاں** (Haan) - I am
- **او** (O) - You are (informal)
- **اے** (Ae) - He/She/It is
- **آں** (Aan) - We are
- **او** (O) - You are (plural)
- **نیں** (Nain) - They are

## Cultural Context

### When to Use:
- **السلام علیکم** - Formal greeting, any time
- **ابا** - Father (more affectionate than باپ)
- **چاہ** - Tea (common in villages)
- **نئیں** - No (casual, common in speech)

### Regional Notes:
- Pakistani Punjab uses Shahmukhi (Arabic script)
- More similar to Urdu than Indian Punjabi
- Influenced by Arabic and Persian
- Official in Pakistani Punjab province
